Postdoc at Institut Pasteur

Computational Modelling of Brain Folding

Check our BlueSky thread about this position

Location Paris, France
Host Laboratory of Applied and Theoretical Neuroanatomy, Institut Pasteur
Duration 2 years, full-time, with potential renewal
Start Date Flexible (earliest: tomorrow)

About the Role
The Laboratory of Applied and Theoretical Neuroanatomy, directed by Dr. Roberto Toro at Institut Pasteur, is seeking a motivated postdoctoral researcher to work on the computational modelling of brain folding. This position is part of the ERC Synergy project Unfold, which brings together an international team to decipher the causes and consequences of brain folding.

Key Qualifications
  • Neuroscientist with a quantitative background (physics, applied mathematics, engineering).
  • Knowledge of continuum mechanics, numerical simulation, strong programming skills and experience in numerical computing and mesh processing.
  • Deeply interested in brain development, morphogenesis, and the role of mechanical forces in biology.
What We Offer
  • A dynamic, interdisciplinary lab in the heart of Paris.
  • Access to world-class facilities at Institut Pasteur.
  • Opportunities for collaboration, conferences, and professional growth within an international research network.
How to Apply
Send the following documents to rto@pasteur.fr:
  • CV including a publication list.
  • Cover letter highlighting your research interests and fit for the position.
  • Contact information for three references.

Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis

25 November 2024

Postdoc at Institut Pasteur

Multimodal Atlas of Brain Folding Development

Check our BlueSky thread about this position

Location Paris, France
Host Laboratory of Applied and Theoretical Neuroanatomy, Institut Pasteur
Duration 2 years, full-time, with potential renewal
Start Date Flexible (earliest: tomorrow)

About the Role
The Laboratory of Applied and Theoretical Neuroanatomy, led by Dr. Roberto Toro at Institut Pasteur, is hiring a postdoctoral researcher to construct a multimodal, multi-scale atlas of brain folding development. This role is part of the ERC Synergy project Unfold, which integrates expertise in cellular, molecular, and mechanical processes to uncover the mechanisms of brain folding.

The successful candidate will:
  • Develop a comprehensive developmental atlas of ferret brain folding.
  • Integrate diverse data modalities, including histology, transcription, tissue mechanics, MRI, and polarised light microscopy.
  • Collaborate within a vibrant and interdisciplinary research team in Paris and internationally.
Key Qualifications
  • Neuroscientist with a quantitative background (physics, applied mathematics, engineering).
  • Expertise in computational neuroanatomy, neuroimaging, multimodal data integration, computational tools for mesh processing, and strong programming skills (Python, JavaScript).
  • Passion for understanding brain development, data visualisation and open-science.
What We Offer
  • A collaborative and innovative research environment at Institut Pasteur.
  • Access to state-of-the-art facilities and international collaborations.
  • A vibrant scientific community in one of Europe's most exciting cities.
How to Apply
Send the following documents to rto@pasteur.fr:
  • CV including a publication list.
  • Cover letter highlighting your research interests and fit for the position.
  • Contact information for three references.

Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis
